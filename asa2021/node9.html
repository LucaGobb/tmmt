Arbitrary Sub-Structures

  • Structures that are not necessarily well approximated with piecewise cylinders and/or cones (or even 1D wave propagation) can still be incorporated into a TMM scheme.

  • An approach that has been successfully applied is to use FEM to model such structures and to then derive approximate TM elements from corresponding input/output numerical results (typically with polynomial fittings).

  • Lefebvre and Scavone (2012) used this approach to achieve refined characterization for toneholes.

  • More recently, Wang and Scavone (2021) applied the same approach for a saxophone mouthpiece.

    Figure 14: A finite element representation of a saxophone mouthpiece (from Wang and Scavone (2021)).

  • Wang et al. (2020) also found that a piecewise geometric model of the saxophone mouthpiece produced a very accurate result, negating the need for the FEM.

  • Eveno et al. (2012) compared the modeling of trumpet and trombone horns using short piece-wise cylinders vs. cones and found the spherical-wave results to better match measurements.


Presented at the 180th Meeting of the Acoustical Society of America, 8-10 June 2021 by Gary P. Scavone.
